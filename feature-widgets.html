Feature deep-dives

Marketing and utility widgets

These are the reusable sections you can add to almost any page: pop-ups, sign-up forms, countdowns, testimonials, trust badges, image banners, and more. Here is what each one does and every setting inside it.

Every page on your store is built from stackable blocks called sections. A section is one self-contained band of content, like a banner across the top or a row of testimonials. This page walks through the marketing and utility sections available to you, one by one. For each one you get a short line on what it is for, then a table listing every setting you can change. You will not use all of these, and that is fine. Skim to the widget you need, add it to a page, and fill in only the settings that matter to you.

How to add a section to a page

Adding any of the widgets below follows the same short routine. You do it all in the visual editor, which shows a live preview of your store as you work.

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize. Your store opens in a live preview with an edit panel down the left side.
  2. Pick the page. Use the page dropdown at the top of the editor to switch to the page you want to edit, for example your home page or an About page.
  3. Click Add section. In the left panel, click Add section where you want the new band of content to appear.
  4. Choose the widget. A list of available sections appears. Click the one you want, such as Newsletter or Image banner. It drops into the page straight away.
  5. Fill in the settings. With the section selected, its settings show in the left panel. Work through them using the tables on this page as your guide. The preview updates as you type.
  6. Save. Click Save in the top right. Your changes go live once saved.
Good to know

Many sections let you add repeatable pieces inside them, called blocks. A Testimonials section, for example, holds one block per customer quote. When a widget has blocks, you add them with an Add block button inside that section, then drag them to reorder. Every widget below notes which blocks it offers.

Good to know

Almost every section shares the same spacing controls near the bottom of its settings: Section margin (Top, Bottom, Mobile top, Mobile bottom) and sometimes Section padding. These add or remove empty space around the section on desktop and mobile. To keep the tables short and readable, we mention these once here rather than repeating them for every widget. Adjust them if a section feels too cramped or too spread out.

Pop-ups and alerts

These widgets appear over the top of the page or float in a corner to grab attention. Use them sparingly, since too many at once will annoy shoppers.

Pop-up

A window that appears over the page, often to promote a sign-up or an offer.

SettingWhat it does
ImageAn optional picture to show inside the pop-up.
Image layoutWhere the image sits in relation to the text.
Image fitWhether the image fills its space or shows in full without cropping.
Limit displayHow often the pop-up is shown to the same visitor, so it does not appear on every page view.
Limit display cache lifetime (days)How many days the store remembers that a visitor has already seen the pop-up before it may show again.
PositionWhere on the screen the pop-up appears, for example centered or in a corner.

Triggers control when the pop-up opens and closes.

SettingWhat it does
IdA name for this pop-up, used when you want to open it from a specific button or link.
Trigger openWhat causes the pop-up to appear, such as after a time delay or on scroll.
Time delay for a trigger openThe number of seconds to wait before the pop-up appears.
Open scroll typeWhether scrolling opens the pop-up and how that scroll is measured.
Open scroll positionHow far down the page a visitor must scroll before the pop-up opens.
CSS selector for manual triggerAn advanced field for linking a specific element to open this pop-up. Best left to your developer.
Trigger closeWhat causes the pop-up to close on its own.
Time delay for a trigger closeThe number of seconds before the pop-up closes automatically.
Close scroll typeWhether scrolling closes the pop-up and how that scroll is measured.
Close scroll positionHow far the visitor scrolls before the pop-up closes.

Style controls how the pop-up looks.

SettingWhat it does
WidthHow wide the pop-up window is.
Color schemeThe set of colors used, chosen from your theme's saved color schemes.
Open animationThe effect used as the pop-up appears.
Exit animationThe effect used as the pop-up closes.
Show overlayDims the rest of the page behind the pop-up when turned on.

Advanced holds extra behavior options.

SettingWhat it does
Mobile disableHides the pop-up on phones when turned on.
Disable scrollingStops the page behind the pop-up from scrolling while it is open.
Click overlay to closeLets the visitor close the pop-up by clicking the dimmed area around it.
Press ESC to closeLets the visitor close the pop-up with the Escape key.
Press F4 to closeLets the visitor close the pop-up with the F4 key.

Blocks you can add inside a pop-up: Heading (a title), Description (a line of text), Subscribe form (an email sign-up box with its own placeholder text, button label, button style, and description), and Social media links (checkboxes to show icons for Twitter, Facebook, Pinterest, Instagram, TikTok, Tumblr, Snapchat, YouTube, and Vimeo).

Take care

A pop-up interrupts the shopper, so set a sensible display limit and delay. Showing it too early or on every visit tends to drive people away rather than win sign-ups.

Age verification popup

A gate that asks visitors to confirm their age before entering the store.

Question is the prompt shown first.

SettingWhat it does
HeadingThe main question, for example asking the visitor to confirm their age.
Heading sizeHow large that heading appears.
TextSupporting text shown below the heading.
Decline button labelThe wording on the button a visitor clicks if they do not meet the age requirement.
Approve button labelThe wording on the button a visitor clicks to confirm and enter.

Declined is what shows if the visitor says no.

SettingWhat it does
HeadingThe message shown to a visitor who declines.
TextSupporting text shown to a visitor who declines.

Notification banner

A small message that floats in a corner of the screen, handy for announcements or a link to an offer.

SettingWhat it does
LayoutThe overall arrangement of the banner.
ImageAn optional picture to show in the banner.
Image fitWhether the image fills its space or shows in full without cropping.
Image positionWhere the image sits within the banner.
HeadingThe banner's title.
Heading sizeHow large the heading appears.
TextThe banner's message.
Button labelThe wording on the banner's button.
Button actionWhat the button does when clicked.
Button linkThe page the button opens.
Button styleThe look of the button.
Limit displayHow often the banner is shown to the same visitor.
Limit display cache lifetime (days)How many days the store remembers a visitor has seen the banner before it may show again.
PositionWhich corner or edge of the screen the banner sits in.

The notification banner also carries the same Triggers, Style, and Advanced controls described above for the pop-up: Id, Trigger open and its delay, open and close scroll settings, a CSS selector for manual triggering, Trigger close and its delay, Width, Color scheme, Open and Exit animation, Show overlay, Mobile disable, Disable scrolling, Click overlay to close, Press ESC to close, and Press F4 to close.

Sign-ups and urgency

These widgets help you grow your email list and highlight time-limited moments.

Newsletter

An email sign-up band that invites shoppers to subscribe to your list.

SettingWhat it does
Color schemeThe set of colors used for the section.
Content vertical alignmentWhether the text sits at the top, middle, or bottom of the band.
HeadingThe invitation title, for example a prompt to join your mailing list.
Heading sizeHow large the heading appears.
DescriptionA short line explaining why to sign up.
Button styleThe look of the sign-up button.
Show button dotAdds a small decorative dot to the button when turned on.

This section also carries the standard spacing controls: Section margin (Top, Bottom, Mobile top, Mobile bottom) and Section padding (Top, Bottom).

Good to know

Everyone who signs up through this form is saved in your Customers area, where they are marked as accepting email marketing. You can then reach them through your email tool.

Countdown

A ticking timer that counts down to a set date and time, good for sales or launches.

SettingWhat it does
End dateThe date the timer counts down to.
End timeThe time of day the timer reaches zero.
Date font sizeHow large the counting numbers appear.
After the timer endsWhat happens once the countdown reaches zero, such as hiding the timer.
TextA line of text shown alongside the timer.

Section heading options add a title above the timer.

SettingWhat it does
HeadingThe section title.
Heading sizeHow large the heading appears.
SubheadingA smaller line above or below the heading.
Show subheading dotAdds a small decorative dot next to the subheading.
DescriptionSupporting text for the section.

Button options add a call to action.

SettingWhat it does
LabelThe wording on the button.
LinkThe page the button opens.
Show button dotAdds a small decorative dot to the button.
Button styleThe look of the button.

Trust and social proof

These widgets reassure shoppers by showing badges, ratings, and customer feedback.

Trust badges

A row of small logos or icons, such as payment or certification badges, to build confidence.

SettingWhat it does
SubheadingA smaller line above the heading.
HeadingThe section title.
Heading sizeHow large the heading appears.

Under Section spacing you also have Top spacing, Bottom spacing, Mobile top spacing, and Mobile bottom spacing.

Blocks you can add: Badge, up to six of them. Each badge has a Badge image and Alt text, which is a short written description of the image for accessibility.

Trustpilot

Displays your reviews from Trustpilot, the third-party review service, if you have a Trustpilot account.

SettingWhat it does
HeadingThe section title shown above the reviews.
Business Unit IDYour account identifier from Trustpilot, which connects the widget to your reviews.
Widget template IDWhich Trustpilot review layout to show, supplied by Trustpilot.
Trustpilot review domainThe website address registered with your Trustpilot account.
LocaleThe language and region for the reviews display.
Widget themeThe light or dark look of the review widget.
Widget heightHow tall the review widget is.
Color schemeThe set of colors used for the surrounding section.
Good to know

The Business Unit ID, template ID, and domain all come from your Trustpilot account. If you do not use Trustpilot, you can skip this widget. Ask your developer if you need help finding these values.

Testimonials

A sliding set of customer quotes, each with an optional photo and star rating.

Section heading options add a title.

SettingWhat it does
HeadingThe section title.
Heading sizeHow large the heading appears.
SubheadingA smaller line above or below the heading.
Show subheading dotAdds a small decorative dot next to the subheading.

Slider options control how the quotes cycle.

SettingWhat it does
Enable autoplayCycles through the quotes on its own when turned on.
Disable sliding on hoverPauses the automatic cycling while a visitor's cursor is over it.
Speed slidingHow quickly one quote slides to the next.
Delay slidingHow long each quote stays on screen before moving on.
Button styleThe look of the arrows or navigation buttons.

Blocks you can add: Testimonials slide, one per quote. Each slide has an Image, the quote Text, the Author name, the Author position (their job or company), an Enable stars toggle, and a Review score (the star rating).

Image collage

A styled arrangement of images with room for text and a link, often used for a feature or highlight.

SettingWhat it does

Gallery carousel

A single sliding strip of images that shoppers move through one at a time.

Section heading options: Heading, Heading size, Subheading, Show subheading dot, Show border, and Aspect ratio for the image shape.

Slider options: Speed sliding for how fast the images move, and Enable stop button to let visitors pause the slider.

Blocks you can add: Carousel slide, one per image. Each block holds a single Slide image.

Text and columns

These widgets place written content on a page, from a single passage to columns and expandable rows.

Rich text

A block of formatted text with an optional heading, good for an About passage or a policy summary.

SettingWhat it does
Section layoutHow the text block is arranged on the page.
SubheadingA smaller line above the heading.
HeadingThe section title.
Heading sizeHow large the heading appears.
Show subheading dotAdds a small decorative dot next to the subheading.
DescriptionThe main body text, with formatting such as bold, lists, and links.

Multicolumn

A row of columns, each with its own icon, heading, text, and button. Good for listing features or services side by side.

Section heading options: Heading, Heading size, Subheading, and Show subheading dot.

SettingWhat it does
Columns per rowHow many columns sit side by side.
Content alignmentHow the text in each column is aligned.
Enable swipe (mobile)Lets shoppers swipe through the columns on a phone.
Card heading sizeHow large each column's heading appears.
Card layoutThe arrangement of content within each column.

Blocks you can add: Column, up to twelve. Each column has an Icon, a Heading, a Description with formatting, and button settings (Label, Link, Show button dot, Button style, and Overlay link to make the whole column clickable).

Collapsible content

A set of expandable rows, ideal for a list of frequently asked questions where each answer opens when clicked.

SettingWhat it does
LayoutThe overall arrangement of the rows.
Icon StyleThe symbol used to show a row is open or closed, such as a plus or an arrow.

Heading options: Heading, Heading size, Subheading, and Show subheading dot.

Button options: Label, Link, Show button dot, and Button style.

Blocks you can add: Row, one per question. Each row has a Heading (the question), a Description (the answer, with basic formatting), and an Open by default toggle that shows the answer already expanded.

Do this

Collapsible content is the tidiest way to handle a long list of questions or specifications. It keeps the page short by hiding the detail until a shopper asks for it. Reach for it whenever you have several short question-and-answer pairs.
