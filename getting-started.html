What Shopify is

Shopify is the service that hosts and runs your online store, so you do not have to worry about servers or software. The public website your customers visit is wtfarley.com. The private place where you manage everything behind the scenes is called the admin. Your store runs on Shopify Plus, which is Shopify's top plan, so you have the fullest set of tools available.

Logging in

Everything you do starts by signing in to the admin. Here is how.

  1. Go to the admin address. In your web browser, visit admin.shopify.com, or type your store address followed by /admin.
  2. Enter your email and password. Type the Shopify login email and password that were set up for you, then continue.
  3. Approve the verification code if asked. For extra security, Shopify may send you a two-step verification code. Enter it to finish signing in.
Good to know

Once you are in, add the admin to your browser bookmarks. That way you can get back to it in one click every time, without needing to remember the address.

Take care

Never share your login with anyone. If a colleague needs access, give them their own account with the right level of permission from Settings›Users and permissions. Individual accounts keep your store safe and let you see who changed what.

A tour of the admin

The admin has a steady layout that stays the same wherever you go, so once it feels familiar you will always know where you are.

  • Down the left-hand side is the main navigation menu: Home, Orders, Products, Customers, Content, Marketing, Discounts, and Analytics.
  • Below that is a Sales channels area, which includes your Online Store.
  • At the very bottom left sits Settings, where the behind-the-scenes options live.
  • Running across the top is a search bar you can use to jump straight to a product, order, or customer.
Good to know

The first thing you see after logging in is your Home dashboard. It gives you a friendly overview: recent activity, a snapshot of how the store is doing, and any tasks that need your attention. It is a good place to land each day before you dive into a task.

Saving your work

This is the single most important habit to build. Whenever you make a change in the admin, a Save button appears near the top right of the screen. Nothing you type or change goes live until you press it. If you move to another screen or close the tab without saving, your changes are lost.

Do

Get into the habit of pressing Save the moment you finish a change. When in doubt, save. It is the safest thing you can do.

Active, Draft, and publishing

Saving keeps your work. Publishing is what decides whether customers can see it. A few different parts of your store have a visible-versus-hidden state, and it helps to understand them together.

Products use a status label:

  • Active means the product is visible on wtfarley.com and can be bought.
  • Draft means the product is hidden from customers while you work on it.

Pages (like About Us) and your store design (the theme) work the same way in spirit: each has a published state that customers see and a hidden state that only you see. This lets you prepare things quietly and reveal them when they are ready.

Good to know

To hide a product temporarily, set it to Draft rather than deleting it. Deleting removes it for good, while Draft simply tucks it out of sight so you can bring it back at any time.

Getting help fast

You are never stuck for long. When you need a hand, here are the quickest ways to get one.

  • The Shopify Help Center at help.shopify.com has clear, searchable guides for almost anything.
  • Inside the admin, look for the "?" and the search bar to find answers without leaving the page.
  • The Shopify mobile app (for iOS and Android) lets you check orders and keep an eye on the store while you are away from your desk.
Take care

For anything that involves Edit code, the structure of metaobjects, or the checkout, please contact your developer before changing it. These are the sensitive parts of the store, and a quick message first will save you time and worry.
