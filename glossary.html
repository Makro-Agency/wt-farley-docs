Glossary and FAQ

Plain-English definitions of every Shopify and WT Farley term used in this guide, plus quick answers to common questions.

Glossary

New words come up quickly when you start managing a store. Here is every term used in this guide, explained simply and listed in alphabetical order. Where a term has its own page, the term links to it.

TermWhat it means
Active / Draft Whether a product is visible to shoppers (Active) or hidden while you finish working on it (Draft). See Products.
Admin The behind-the-scenes area where you manage your store. Shoppers never see it. See Getting around Shopify.
Archive A way to hide a product from your store while keeping its full record for later. See Products.
Base price The starting price of a configurator product before any options are added on.
B2B Business-to-business selling, where companies buy through their own accounts and see wholesale prices.
Collection A category page that groups related products together, usually filled automatically by tags. See Pages & navigation.
Compare-at price An original price shown with a line through it, next to the current price, to signal a saving.
Configurator (composite) product A build-your-own product that shoppers assemble from option menus, for example the Oxygen Hose. See Configurator products.
Default choice The option that is already selected when a configurator product first loads on the page.
Fulfil To mark an order as shipped, which notifies the customer that their items are on the way.
Handle The short name of a product or page that appears in its web address.
Media The photos and images shown on a product. See Products.
Metafield An extra custom field on a product, page, or other record. Custom field names look like custom.gas_type.
Metaobject A reusable piece of structured content stored once and shared across many products. The configurator option menus are metaobjects. See Configurator products.
Option group One menu of choices on a configurator product, for example Hose Length.
Option choice A single selectable option inside a group, for example 10 Feet.
Part code The short code that an option choice adds to the built part number.
Part number position The order in which a group's code appears within the final built part number.
Price delta The amount an option choice adds to, or subtracts from, the price of a build.
Publish To make something live and visible on your store.
Sales channel A place where you sell, such as the Online Store. See Getting around Shopify.
SKU The stock keeping unit, your own internal code for a sellable item.
Status Whether a product is currently Active, Draft, or Archived. See Products.
Tag A keyword on a product that, among other things, places it into collections. See Pages & navigation.
Theme The design and layout of your storefront, the part shoppers actually see.
Variant One buyable version of a product that has options, each with its own price and SKU.
Vendor The brand or supplier of a product, which for your store is WT Farley.

Configurator field names

Your build-your-own products carry a few technical field names behind the scenes. You do not need to memorise these, but it helps to recognise them if you ever come across one. Here is what each holds.

FieldWhat it holds
custom.is_configurable Marks a product as a build-your-own configurator product.
custom.sku_prefix The starting code for that product's built part numbers.
custom.option_groups The list of option menus shown on the product.
custom.excluded_choices Specific choices that are hidden for this one product.
custom.cfg_price_map The pricing table the page uses to total the build instantly.
custom.gas_group The related products offered by the gas-type switcher.
custom.cfg_stack_base The base image that the option layers sit on top of.
Take care

These fields are managed by the configurator setup and keep your build-your-own products working correctly. Only change them by following the steps on the Configurator products page, or together with your developer.

Common questions

Why can't customers see my product?

The most common reason is that the product is set to Draft rather than Active, or it is not published to the Online Store sales channel. Check both on the product page. See Getting around Shopify and Products.

How do I change the price of a build-your-own product?

The price of a configurator product comes from its base price plus the price deltas of the options a shopper selects. You adjust these on the product's option settings, and the pricing table that totals the build is updated as part of that same process. See Configurator products.

I changed one option and it changed several products. Why?

Option menus are shared metaobjects, which means the same menu can be reused across many products. Editing the menu updates every product that uses it. To learn how these are set up, see Configurator products.

How do I put a product into a category?

Add the matching tag to the product, and the collection that looks for that tag will pick it up automatically. See Pages & navigation and Products.

How do I hide a product for now without losing it?

Set the product to Draft or Archive rather than deleting it. Both keep the full record so you can bring the product back whenever you need it. See Products.

A menu link is wrong. Where do I fix it?

Storefront menus are edited under Content, then Menus, in the admin. See Pages & navigation.

Who do I contact for anything involving code, checkout, or the configurator engine?

Reach out to your developer for anything touching code, the checkout, or the configurator engine. It is best to keep your own changes to content and products, which this guide covers throughout.
