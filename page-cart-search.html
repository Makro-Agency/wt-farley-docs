The cart and search pages

How the shopping cart, the slide-out cart drawer, and the search results page work, and every setting you can adjust on them.

Jump straight to this in your store
Edit the cart Edit the search page
These open your Shopify admin in a new browser tab. You may be asked to log in. For the full list, see Store quick-links.

This page covers three parts of your store that help a shopper find what they need and buy it. The cart is where a shopper reviews everything before they pay. The cart drawer is the small panel that slides in when they add something, without leaving the page they are on. The search results page is what a shopper sees after they type a search. It also explains the newsletter sign-up box that appears at the bottom of the search page and several other pages. You do not need to read this end to end. The cart page

The cart is where a shopper reviews the items they have chosen before they check out. It lists each product, the quantity, and the running total, and it holds the button that starts checkout. Your store has two ways of showing the cart, and you choose which one your shoppers get.

The full cart page versus the cart drawer

There are two styles of cart, and they are an either or choice for the whole store.

  • The full cart page is a normal web page at its own address. When a shopper adds an item, they are taken to this page to review everything.
  • The cart drawer is a small panel that slides in from the side of the screen when a shopper adds an item. They can keep browsing without leaving the page they are on. This is described in its own section below.

You pick between these in your Theme settings, using the Cart type setting described a little further down. The settings in this first section control the full cart page itself.

Good to know

Whichever style you choose, there is always a full cart page a shopper can reach by clicking the cart icon in the header. The drawer is an added convenience on top of that page, not a replacement for it.

Settings on the cart page

These are the settings on the cart section itself. To reach them, open the customizer, then in the top bar switch to your cart page and click the Items section.

Turn the cart note on

  1. Open the customizer. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Go to the cart page. In the bar across the top, open the page menu and choose your cart page.
  3. Open the Items section. Click the Items section in the left panel.
  4. Tick Enable cart note. Turn on Enable cart note.
  5. Save. Click Save in the top right.

The free shipping message here is only a message shown in the cart. It does not by itself make shipping free. The actual free shipping rate is set up in your shipping settings. If you use this message, make sure the amount you type matches a real free shipping rule so shoppers are not misled.

The cart drawer

The cart drawer is the small panel that slides in from the side of the screen the moment a shopper adds something to their cart. It lets them see what is in their cart, adjust quantities, and head to checkout, all without leaving the page they were reading.

Here is what a shopper sees. They click Add to cart on a product, and the drawer glides in showing the item they just added, the other items already in the cart, and the total. They can change a quantity, remove an item, or click through to checkout. If they would rather keep shopping, they close the drawer and carry on exactly where they left off.

Whether shoppers get the drawer or the full cart page is set by the Cart type setting in your Theme settings, described in the cart section above. The colours, subtitle, and empty message you see in the drawer come from the theme-level Cart settings in that same section.

Good to know

The drawer itself does not have its own separate set of editable settings to open and change. It is styled by the theme-level Cart settings covered above, so adjust those to change how the drawer looks and reads.

The search results page

The search results page is what a shopper sees after they type words into the search box and press enter. It lists the products, pages, and blog posts that match what they typed, and it can offer filters and a sort order to help them narrow things down.

How a shopper searches

A shopper clicks the search icon in the header and types what they are looking for, for example a product name or a keyword. As they type, a small preview of likely matches can appear beneath the box. This preview is called predictive search. When they choose a result or press enter, they land on the full search results page described here.

Good to know

Predictive search is the live list of suggestions that drops down while a shopper is still typing, before they have finished. It saves them a step by offering the most likely matches straight away. The full search results page is the complete list they reach once they press enter or click through.

Good to know

WT Farley also has a separate part-number lookup built into the header, for customers who already know the exact WT Farley part number they want. That is different from ordinary search. It is covered on the custom features page.

Show or hide the SKU on results

  1. Open the customizer. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Go to the search page. In the top bar, open the page menu and choose the search page. You can type a search in the preview to see results.
  3. Open Search results. Click the Search results section in the left panel.
  4. Tick or untick Show SKU. Turn Show SKU on to show part codes, or off to hide them.
  5. Save. Click Save.

These Search settings include some behind-the-scenes options for the part-number lookup that your developer set up. Those are not everyday settings to change yourself. If the lookup ever needs adjusting, please pass it to your developer. You can read more about the lookup on the custom features page.

Turn predictive search on or off

  1. Open the customizer. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Open Theme settings. Click the gear icon at the bottom of the left panel.
  3. Choose Search. Open the Search group of settings.
  4. Set Enable predictive search. Tick it to show live suggestions as shoppers type, or untick it to turn them off.
  5. Save. Click Save.

The newsletter sign-up

The newsletter sign-up is a box that invites shoppers to enter their email address so you can keep in touch, for example with product news or offers. It appears at the bottom of your search page and on several other pages of your store. Anyone who signs up is added to your customer list so you can email them later.

You edit it in the customizer by clicking the Newsletter section on whichever page it appears.

Online Store›Themes›Customize

SettingWhat it does
Color schemeSets the colours of the sign-up box, chosen from your saved colour schemes.
Content vertical alignmentPositions the text and box towards the top, middle, or bottom of the section.
HeadingThe main line of text, for example an invitation to join your mailing list.
Heading sizeSets how large that heading appears.
DescriptionA short line under the heading explaining what shoppers get by signing up.
Button styleChooses the look of the sign-up button so it matches the rest of your store.
Show button dotAdds a small decorative dot to the button. A styling touch only.
Top, Bottom (Section margin)The space above and below the section on a computer screen.
Mobile top, Mobile bottomThe same spacing, set separately for phones and small screens.
Top, Bottom (Section padding)The space inside the section, above and below its content.

Change the newsletter wording

  1. Open the customizer. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Find the Newsletter section. Go to a page that shows it, such as the search page, and click the Newsletter section in the left panel.
  3. Edit the text. Update the Heading and Description to the wording you want.
  4. Save. Click Save.
Good to know

Everyone who signs up through this box appears in your Customers area, marked as having agreed to marketing email. You can see and manage that list from Customers in the main admin.
