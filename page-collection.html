Editing each page

Collection and category pages

Category pages have two parts: a banner at the top and the product grid below it, with filters and sorting. This page covers every setting on both, plus the page that lists all your collections.

Jump straight to this in your store
Edit the collection layout Collections
These open your Shopify admin in a new browser tab. You may be asked to log in. For the full list, see Store quick-links.

A collection page (you may also hear it called a category page) is any page that shows a group of products together, for example all your oxygen hoses or all your carts. Every collection page in your store shares the same layout, so once you learn it here you know them all.

There are two very different kinds of editing on these pages, and it helps to keep them apart in your mind:

  • Layout and settings. This is the shape of the page: how many products sit in a row, whether filters appear, whether the banner shows an image, and so on. You change these in the theme editor, and your change applies to every collection page at once.
  • A collection's own content and products. This is the title, description, and image of one particular collection, plus which products belong in it. You change these under Products›Collections, and your change affects only that one collection. For how to build and fill a collection, see the Pages & navigation page.

This page is mostly about the first kind: the layout and settings you reach through the theme editor. Nothing goes live until you press Save.

Good to know

To open the theme editor, follow this path: Online Store›Themes›Customize. Then, using the page selector at the very top of the editor, choose a collection to preview. Every setting you change while viewing that page applies to all collection pages, not just the one on screen.

The collection banner

The banner is the strip at the very top of a collection page. It normally shows the collection's title, and it can also show the collection's description and image. Remember: the words and picture themselves come from the collection you set up under Products›Collections. The settings below simply decide how that content is displayed.

In the theme editor, open a collection page and click the Collection banner section to see these settings.

Header overlayon / off

When on, the collection title (and description) sit on top of the banner image, rather than above or below it. Turn it on when you have a background image and want the text laid over it. Turn it off for a cleaner, text-only look.

Full widthon / off

When on, the banner stretches edge to edge across the screen. When off, it stays within the normal page margins, lined up with the rest of the content.

Color schemecolour set

Chooses which set of background and text colours the banner uses. The available schemes come from your overall theme colours, so this keeps the banner matching the rest of your store.

Overlay opacityslider

Controls how dark the shading is over the banner image. A higher value darkens the image more, which makes overlaid text easier to read. This only matters when Header overlay is on and an image is showing.

Show collection descriptionon / off

When on, the description you wrote for the collection appears in the banner. When off, only the title shows. Turn it on if your collections have helpful intro text you want shoppers to read.

Show collection imageon / off

When on, the collection's image is used as the banner background. When off, the banner has no picture. The image itself is set on the collection under Products›Collections.

Image for "Products" pageimage

Sets a banner image for the special page that lists all products together (the one at /collections/all, which has no image of its own). Click to choose an image from your library or upload one. This is the one place where you pick an image directly in the theme editor rather than on a collection.

Aspect ratiochoice

Sets the shape of the banner image: for example tall, square, or wide. Pick the shape that best frames your banner pictures.

The banner section also has spacing controls, grouped under a Section margin heading: Top and Bottom set the empty space above and below the banner on desktop, and Mobile top and Mobile bottom do the same on phones. In most cases you can leave these at their default and only nudge them if the banner feels too cramped or too spread out.

Change how the banner looks

  1. Open the theme editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. View a collection page. Use the page selector at the top and choose any collection so you can see the banner.
  3. Open the Collection banner section. Click it in the list on the left.
  4. Adjust the settings. For example, turn Show collection description on, or change the Aspect ratio. The preview updates as you go.
  5. Save. Press Save at the top right. The change now applies to every collection page.
Good to know

If the banner looks empty on a particular collection, it usually means that collection has no description or no image yet. Add them under Products›Collections, and they will appear as long as the matching setting above is switched on.

The product grid

The product grid is the main part of the page: the rows of product cards below the banner, along with the filters and sorting controls that help shoppers narrow things down. In the theme editor, click the Product grid section to see its settings.

How many products, and how they page

Products per pageslider

Sets how many products load before the shopper needs to move to the next page or load more. A larger number means more scrolling per page; a smaller number means quicker loading and more pages. A middle value usually feels best.

Pagination typechoice

Decides what happens when a shopper reaches the bottom of the products shown. Depending on the option you pick, they either see numbered pages to click through, or a button to load more products, or products that load automatically as they scroll.

Layout on phones

Under a Mobile layout heading:

Number of columnschoice

Sets how many product cards sit side by side on a phone screen, typically one or two. Two columns show more at a glance; one column makes each product larger and easier to tap.

Good to know

The number of products per row on desktop is worked out automatically by the theme to suit the screen width, so there is no separate desktop columns setting here. The Number of columns setting above controls the phone view, and the image and spacing settings shape how each card looks on both.

What each product card shows

Under a Product card options heading, these settings decide what appears on each little product tile in the grid:

Image ratiochoice

Sets the shape of each product photo in the grid: for example square, portrait, or the image's natural shape. Choosing one shape keeps all your cards looking tidy and even.

Image fitchoice

Decides whether a photo fills the whole card space (which may crop the edges) or fits fully inside it (which may leave a little blank space around it). Use "fit fully inside" if it matters that no part of the product is cut off.

Show second image on hoveron / off

When on, moving the mouse over a card swaps to the product's second photo. A nice touch when your products have a good alternative angle as their second image.

Show vendoron / off

When on, the brand or vendor name appears on the card. For your store the vendor is usually WT Farley, so you may prefer to leave this off to save space.

Show sale badgeon / off

When on, a small "Sale" label appears on any product whose compare-at price is higher than its price. Helps shoppers spot a deal.

Show SKUon / off

When on, each card shows the product's SKU (its internal code). Useful for trade customers who order by part number.

Show collection nameon / off

When on, the card shows which collection the product belongs to. Usually best left off on a collection page, since the shopper is already inside a collection.

Show descriptionon / off

When on, a short line of product description appears on the card. Keep in mind this comes from the product's short card text, so it works best when that text is kept brief.

Show footer SEO copyon / off

When on, any extra descriptive text set at the bottom of the collection is shown beneath the grid. This is copy that helps the page perform in search results. Leave it on unless you have a reason to hide it.

Filters and sorting

Filters and sorting are the tools shoppers use to find what they want within a collection. Filters appear as tick boxes or menus (for example by gas type, by price range) that let a shopper narrow the list to only matching products. Sorting is a menu that reorders the products, for example price low to high, or newest first. Under a Filtering and sorting heading you control both:

Enable filteringon / off

When on, shoppers can filter the products on the page. Leave this on so customers can quickly narrow a long list. The actual filter options (such as gas type) are set up separately in your admin under Search and discovery.

Enable sortingon / off

When on, shoppers get the "Sort by" menu to reorder products. Best left on.

Show sidebar filteron / off

When on, the filters sit in a column down the side of the page rather than in a bar across the top. Sidebar filters suit collections with lots of filter choices; a top bar keeps things compact.

Show grid / list view toggleon / off

When on, shoppers get a small control to switch between the tile grid and a stacked list view. Some customers prefer a list when comparing many similar products.

The image filter

Under an Image filter heading, these settings control filters that are shown as pictures rather than plain text (for example a set of thumbnails a shopper can click):

Image ratiochoice

Sets the shape of each filter picture.

Image fitchoice

Decides whether each filter picture fills its space or fits fully inside it, just like the product card setting above.

Image per rowslider

Sets how many filter pictures sit side by side.

As with the banner, the grid section ends with Section margin spacing controls: Top and Bottom for desktop, and Mobile top and Mobile bottom for phones. Leave these at their defaults unless the grid feels too tight or too loose.

Change how many products show per row

  1. Open the theme editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize and view any collection page.
  2. Open the Product grid section.
  3. For phones, set Number of columns under Mobile layout to one or two.
  4. For the overall feel, adjust Products per page and the Image ratio, which together change how packed each row looks.
  5. Save. Press Save. The change applies to every collection page.

Turn filters on or off

  1. Open the theme editor and view a collection page.
  2. Open the Product grid section.
  3. Find the Filtering and sorting heading.
  4. Switch Enable filtering on or off. While you are there, decide whether you want Show sidebar filter on for a side column or off for a top bar.
  5. Save.
Good to know

Turning Enable filtering on shows the filter tools, but which filters actually appear (price, gas type, and so on) is managed separately in your admin under Search and discovery. If a filter you expect is missing, that is where it is set up, not in the theme editor.

The all-collections page

Besides the individual category pages, your store has one page that lists all of your collections together, each shown as a clickable card. Shoppers use it as a directory to jump into whichever category they want. In the theme editor, choose the collections list page from the page selector, then click the Collections list page section to see these settings.

Headingtext

The title shown at the top of the page, for example "Shop by category". Type whatever wording suits your store.

Section layoutchoice

Sets the overall arrangement of the collection cards on the page. Pick the layout that presents your categories most clearly.

Heading sizechoice

Controls how large the main page heading appears. This is the size of the "Heading" text above.

Color schemecolour set

Chooses which set of theme colours the page uses for its background and text, keeping it in step with the rest of your store.

Collections per pageslider

Sets how many collection cards load before the shopper moves to a further page. If you have only a handful of collections, set this high enough to show them all at once.

Columns per rowslider

Sets how many collection cards sit side by side across the page. Fewer columns make each card larger; more columns fit more categories in view.

Sort collections bychoice

Decides the order the collections appear in, for example alphabetically by name, or by the order you have arranged them. Choose the order that helps shoppers find categories fastest.

Heading sizechoice

A second heading size setting, this one for the title shown on each individual collection card, so you can make the category names on the cards larger or smaller.

Layout on phones

Under a Mobile layout heading:

Enable swipeon / off

When on, shoppers on a phone can swipe sideways through the collection cards instead of scrolling down through them. A tidy way to present several categories in a small space.

The collection images

Under an Image options heading, these settings shape the picture on each collection card:

Image sourcechoice

Decides which picture each card uses, for example the image you set on the collection itself, or the photo of the first product in it. Setting your own collection images gives the tidiest result.

Fit collection image into containerchoice

Decides whether each card image fills its space (which may crop it) or fits fully inside it (which may leave a little blank space). Use "fit fully inside" if it matters that the whole image is visible.

Aspect ratiochoice

Sets the shape of the card images, for example square or wide. Keeping one shape makes the grid of cards look even.

The shading over the images

Under an Overlay options heading:

Enableon / off

When on, a shaded layer sits over each card image, which helps the collection name stand out on top of a busy picture.

Opacityslider

Sets how dark that shading is. A higher value darkens the image more and makes the text easier to read. This only matters when the overlay above is on.

As with the other pages, this section ends with Section margin spacing: Top and Bottom for desktop, and Mobile top and Mobile bottom for phones.

Rename the all-collections page heading

  1. Open the theme editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. View the collections list page. Use the page selector at the top to choose it.
  3. Open the Collections list page section.
  4. Edit the Heading. Type your new wording, and adjust Columns per row if you want the cards larger or smaller.
  5. Save.
Do

To decide which collections exist in the first place, and to give each one a good name and image, work under Products›Collections. The Pages & navigation page walks you through creating collections and choosing the products inside them.
