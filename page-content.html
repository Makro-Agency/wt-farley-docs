Editing each page

Content pages

Your store has several page layouts: a standard page, plus custom designs for About, Contact, FAQ, and the dealer sign-up. This page explains each layout and the sections that build them.

Every page in your store is made of two things: the words and the layout. It helps to keep them separate in your mind, because you edit them in two different places.

The words of a page, its title and body text, live under Content›Pages. That is where you go to reword your company story or fix a typo. The Pages and navigation page of this guide walks through that editor step by step.

The layout of a page, meaning the visual arrangement of banners, images, and text blocks, is called a template. A template is built from sections, and you arrange those sections in the theme editor at Online Store›Themes›Customize. This is where the settings below live.

Most pages use the same simple layout. A few important pages, like About and Contact, have their own custom layouts built from richer sections. The rest of this page explains each one.

Good to know

A recurring group of settings called Section margin appears on nearly every section: Top, Bottom, Mobile top, and Mobile bottom. These simply control the empty space above and below the section, with separate values for large screens and for phones. You can leave them at their defaults. We describe them once here and note where they appear rather than repeat them for every section.

The standard page

Most of your pages, including Terms and Conditions and Your Privacy Choices, use the standard page layout. It is the plainest option: it takes the title and body text you typed under Content›Pages and displays them cleanly, with nothing else added. This is the right choice for straightforward text content.

The layout is built from a single section called Page. It has very few settings, because the words themselves come from the page content, not from the theme editor.

The Page section

SettingWhat it does
Heading sizeChoose how large the page title appears at the top of the page.
Section margin (Top, Bottom, Mobile top, Mobile bottom)The spacing above and below the content, as described in the note above.
Do

For a plain text page, write everything in the Content box under Content›Pages. The Page section will display it automatically. You rarely need to open the theme editor for a standard page at all.

How to create a new basic page

Content›Pages

  1. Open your pages. In the left menu of your admin, click Content, then click Pages.
  2. Add a page. Click Add page in the top right.
  3. Give it a title. Type a title in the Title field. This becomes the headline shoppers see and part of the page's web address.
  4. Write the content. Type your text in the large Content box. Use the toolbar to make text bold, add headings, create lists, or add links, much like a word processor.
  5. Confirm it is visible. Set Visibility to Visible so shoppers can see it.
  6. Save. Click Save in the top right. The new page uses the standard page layout automatically.
Good to know

To link people to a new page, add it to a menu under Content›Menus. See the Pages and navigation page for how menus work.

The About page

The About page tells your company story, so it uses a richer custom layout instead of the plain standard page. It is built by stacking several sections one after another. From top to bottom these are a page hero, an image with text, a rich text block, a multicolumn row, and a set of trust badges. You can reorder, hide, or remove any of them in the theme editor.

Three of these sections are documented below. The Multicolumn and Trust badges sections are shared marketing widgets used across the store, so they are covered on the Marketing widgets page rather than repeated here.

Page hero

The hero is the banner strip at the very top of the page. It introduces the page with a large heading and an optional short description.

How to edit the text on the About page

Online Store›Themes›Customize

  1. Open the theme editor. Click Online Store, then Themes, then the Customize button.
  2. Switch to the About page. At the very top of the editor there is a page selector, usually showing "Home page". Click it, choose Pages, and select your About page so its layout loads in the preview.
  3. Pick a section. In the left panel you will see the sections that build the page, such as Page hero and Image with text. Click the one you want to change.
  4. Edit its text. Update the Heading, Description, and other fields on the right. Your changes appear instantly in the preview.
  5. Save. Click Save in the top right. Your changes are now live.

The Contact page

The Contact page uses the standard Page section for any introductory text, plus a dedicated Contact form section that gives shoppers a form to fill in and send to you.

Where do messages go?

When a shopper fills in the contact form and clicks send, their message is emailed to your store's contact email address. You set that address under Settings›General, in the Store contact email field. If messages are not arriving, check that this address is correct and remember to look in your spam folder.

How to change the departments shown on the form

Online Store›Themes›Customize

  1. Open the theme editor and switch to your Contact page using the page selector at the top.
  2. Click the Contact form section in the left panel.
  3. Edit the department options. In the Department options box, type each department on its own line, for example Sales on one line and Support on the next.
  4. Save. Click Save. The drop-down on the live form updates.

The FAQ page

The FAQ page uses the standard Page section plus a Custom FAQ section. The Custom FAQ section shows your questions and answers as an accordion: each question is a bar the shopper clicks to expand and reveal its answer, which keeps a long list tidy.

Each question (accordion row)

Inside the Custom FAQ section, every question is its own block called a Row. You add one Row per question. Each Row has these settings:

SettingWhat it does
HeadingThe question itself, shown on the clickable bar, for example "Do you ship internationally?".
DescriptionThe answer that appears when the question is expanded. This is a rich text field, so you can add links or bold text.
Open by defaultA checkbox. When ticked, this question starts already expanded so its answer is visible without clicking.

How to add an FAQ question

Online Store›Themes›Customize

  1. Open the theme editor and switch to your FAQ page using the page selector at the top.
  2. Click the Custom FAQ section in the left panel. Its list of question rows appears beneath it.
  3. Add a row. Click Add block (it may be labelled Add accordion or Add row) to create a new question.
  4. Type the question. Put the question in the Heading field.
  5. Type the answer. Put the answer in the Description field.
  6. Save. Click Save. The new question appears on the live page.

How to edit or reorder a question

  1. Open the question. In the theme editor, click the row for the question you want to change.
  2. Edit its text. Update the Heading or Description on the right.
  3. Reorder if needed. Drag a row up or down in the list to change the order questions appear in.
  4. Save. Click Save.

The Become a Dealer page

The Become a WT Farley Dealer page invites businesses to apply to sell your products. It uses the standard Page section for the introduction, plus the same Contact form section as your Contact page, this time set up as a sign-up form.

Because it is the same Contact form section, it has all the settings listed under the Contact page above. The difference is only in how you word it. Applications submitted through it arrive at the same store contact email as your regular contact messages.

Good to know

To make this form read like a dealer application rather than a general enquiry, adjust the wording in the theme editor: change the Heading to something like "Apply to become a dealer", update the Button label to "Submit application", and use the Department options or Subject fields to capture the details you need. Every field is described in the Contact form table above.

Flexible landing pages

Alongside the fixed layouts above, your store also has a flexible landing layout. Think of it as a blank canvas for a special page, such as a seasonal promotion or a campaign, where you can stack whichever marketing sections you like in any order.

This layout can use any of the store's marketing sections, including the image with text and rich text blocks described above, as well as the shared widgets like multicolumn and trust badges. Two guide pages cover your options in full:

  • Marketing widgets explains the shared promotional sections, such as multicolumn and trust badges, and their settings.
  • Section catalog lists every section available in the theme, so you can see what you can add to a page.
Do

When building a landing page, open it in the theme editor, then use Add section to stack the blocks you want. Save often, and preview the page before you link to it from a menu.
