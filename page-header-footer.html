Editing each page

Header, menu and footer

The parts of the site that appear on every page: the header with its logo and announcement bar, the large category side menu, and the footer. Here is every setting on each.

Some parts of your store repeat on every single page. The announcement bar is the thin strip of text at the very top. The header sits just below it, carrying your logo and the search, account and basket icons. Down the left of your category pages runs the big side menu that lists every product department. And at the bottom of every page is the footer, with your contact details, link columns, payment icons and more.

All four are edited in the same visual editor: the theme Customize screen. It shows your real store on the right and a list of its parts on the left, and nothing you change becomes public until you press Save. That makes it a safe place to try things. This page walks through every setting you will find on each part.

Two places, one result

The look of these areas (colours, logo, which settings are switched on) lives in the Customize editor described here. The actual links inside the menus (which categories and pages appear) live in a separate area called Content › Menus. This page covers the settings. For editing the links themselves, see Pages and navigation.

Opening the Customize editor

Every setting on this page is reached the same way, so here is the route once. Whenever a task below shows this chip, this is what it means.

Online Store›Themes›Customize

  1. Open your online store. In the left menu of your admin, click Online Store, then click Themes.
  2. Start customizing. On your current theme, click the Customize button. The visual editor opens with your store shown on the right.
  3. Pick a part to edit. On the left is a list of the parts that make up the page. The header, announcement bar and footer are grouped near the top and bottom of this list. You can also click a part directly in the preview to jump to its settings.
  4. Save when done. Nothing goes live until you click Save in the top right. Until then you are only previewing.
Good to know

The header, announcement bar and footer live in what Shopify calls "section groups", which simply means they appear on every page rather than on one page only. That is why editing them once updates the whole site at the same time.

The header

The header is the bar across the top of every page. It holds your logo, the main navigation, and the search, account and basket icons that shoppers use to move around and check out. In your theme the header is designed to work as-is, so it does not expose extra style settings of its own in the Customize editor. What you control about the header lives in two familiar places instead.

The links in the header

The clickable links across the top (for example About Us and Contact Us) come from a menu, not from the header settings. You add, rename and reorder them under Content › Menus. See Pages and navigation for step by step help.

The logo

Your logo image is uploaded in the footer's Logo info block, described in The footer below, and the theme uses that same image for the site. To swap your logo, follow the "Replace the logo" steps in the footer section.

The search, account and basket icons

These are built into the header and always available to shoppers. There is nothing to switch on or off here, they simply work.

Good to know

Because the header itself has no adjustable settings in the Customize editor, most day to day header work is really menu work. If you want to change what appears across the top, you are looking for Content › Menus, covered in Pages and navigation.

The announcement bar

The announcement bar is the slim strip that sits above the header on every page. It is where you put a short, store-wide message, for example a shipping notice or a phone number for orders. It also has room for one button and a click-to-call phone number. Here is every setting.

Height

Sets how tall the strip is on desktop and larger screens. Drag the slider until the bar looks right around your text.

Height on mobile devices

The same height control, but for phones and small screens. This lets the bar be a comfortable size on mobile without changing the desktop size.

Color scheme

Chooses which of your saved colour combinations the bar uses (its background and text colours). Pick from the list so the bar matches the rest of your branding.

Show announcement text

A tick box that turns the text message on or off. Untick it if you want the bar empty or hidden for a while.

Text

The message itself. This is a small rich text field, so you can make words bold or add a link inside the sentence. Keep it short so it fits on one line.

Button label

Optional. The words shown on a button beside your message, for example "Shop now" or "Contact us". Leave it blank if you do not want a button.

Button link

Where the button takes shoppers when clicked. Choose a page, a collection, or paste a web address. Only used if you have given the button a label above.

Show phone number

A tick box that displays a phone number in the bar. Handy if you want customers to call to order or ask questions.

Phone number (display)

The phone number exactly as you want it to read on screen, including any spaces, dashes or brackets, for example (800) 555 0100.

Phone number (digits only, for tel: link)

The same number with only the digits and no spaces or symbols, for example 8005550100. This is what a phone actually dials when a shopper taps the number on mobile, so type it carefully.

Change the announcement bar message

Online Store›Themes›Customize

  1. Open the editor. Follow the steps in Opening the Customize editor above.
  2. Select the announcement bar. Click it in the preview at the very top of the page, or find Announcement bar in the list on the left.
  3. Make sure it is showing. Check that Show announcement text is ticked.
  4. Edit the text. Type your new message into the Text field. Keep it brief.
  5. Save. Click Save in the top right. The new message is now live on every page.
Good to know

If you set the two phone fields, get them to agree: the display version is what people read, and the digits-only version is what their phone dials. A typo in the digits-only field means the tap-to-call button rings the wrong number.

The category side menu

This is the large product department menu that runs down the side of your store, listing categories like Regulators, Flowmeters, Blenders and Medical Carts. It is the main way shoppers browse your equipment, so it is worth keeping tidy. In the Customize editor this section has one setting.

Menu

This picks which menu the side panel displays. It points to one of your saved menus (your store uses the "sidebar Menu" here). Think of this setting as a socket: it decides which list of links plugs into the panel, but the links themselves are edited elsewhere.

Where the categories come from

The departments and sub-departments you see in this menu are not typed in here. They are the links inside the menu that this setting points to, and you edit them under Content › Menus. To add a new department, rename one, or change its order, see Pages and navigation, which explains menus in full.

Change what appears in the side menu

  1. Editing the links. To add, rename, reorder or nest the categories, go to Content › Menus and edit the sidebar menu. Full steps are in Pages and navigation.
  2. Swapping which menu shows (rare). Only if you want the panel to display a completely different menu, open the Customize editor, select the category side menu section, and change its Menu setting to another menu. Then Save.
Take care

The side menu is your main product navigation, so shoppers rely on it every day. After changing its links in Content › Menus, open your live site and click a few of them to confirm each one lands on the right category page.

The footer

The footer is the wide band at the bottom of every page. It carries your logo and description, contact details, columns of links, social icons, payment icons and the small print at the very bottom. It is built from a set of overall settings plus a handful of blocks you can add. Everything below is edited in the Customize editor.

Overall footer settings

These apply to the footer as a whole.

Color scheme

Chooses the footer's background and text colours from your saved colour combinations, so it matches your branding.

Icon Style

Sets the visual style used for the footer icons (for example the social media icons). Pick the option that looks best with your design.

Show social media icons

A tick box that turns the social media icons on or off. The links behind these icons come from your store's social settings.

Enable country/region selector

Shows a small control letting shoppers choose their country or region. Only useful if your store is set up to sell into more than one region. Leave it off if you sell to one country.

Enable language selector

Shows a control for choosing the site language. Only useful if you have more than one language installed. Leave it off otherwise.

Show payment icons

A tick box that displays the little logos of the payment methods you accept (card brands and so on). These are drawn automatically from what your store accepts at checkout.

Show policy links

A tick box that shows links to your store policies (refund, shipping, privacy, terms) in the footer. The policy wording is edited under Settings › Policies, covered in Pages and navigation.

Enable date in Copyright

Adds the current year to the small copyright line at the very bottom of the footer.

Enable Shop name in Copyright

Adds your store's name to that same copyright line.

Enable Follow on Shop

Shows a "Follow on Shop" button, letting shoppers follow your store in the Shop app. Turn it on if you want to encourage that.

Footer blocks

Beyond the settings above, the footer is filled with blocks. A block is a movable piece you add to the footer, and you can have up to five of them. There are three kinds, and each has its own settings.

BlockWhat it adds to the footer
MenuA column of links, such as your Products, Resources or About columns.
Logo infoYour logo, certification badges, an optional Trustpilot rating, and a short description.
ContactsYour address, phone, email and opening hours.

Menu block

Each Menu block is one column of links in the footer. Add several to build several columns.

Heading

The title shown above the column, for example "Products" or "Resources".

Menu

Picks which saved menu supplies the links in this column. As with the side menu, the links themselves are edited under Content › Menus. See Pages and navigation.

Mobile menu is open by default

On phones, footer columns often collapse to save space. Tick this if you want this particular column already expanded when the page loads on mobile.

Logo info block

This block holds your branding in the footer.

Logo image

Your main logo image. This is the logo the theme uses for the site. Upload a clear image on a transparent or matching background.

Logo image 2

An optional second logo image, if your design calls for two.

Custom logo width

A slider that sets how wide the logo appears. Adjust it until the logo looks balanced rather than too large or too small.

Certification Badge 1 and Certification Badge 2

Two image slots for trust or certification badges you want shown near the logo, for example a quality or industry certification mark.

Show Trustpilot Widget

A tick box that displays a small Trustpilot rating in the footer.

Rating (0 to 5)

The star rating figure to display, for example 4.8. Only used when the Trustpilot widget is switched on.

Reviews label

The short text shown beside the rating, for example "Based on 320 reviews".

Trustpilot page URL

The web address the widget links to, so shoppers can open your full Trustpilot page.

Description

A short sentence or two about your business, shown near the logo. This is a small rich text field.

Contacts block

This block shows how customers reach you.

Address

Your business address. This is a small rich text field, so you can break it across lines.

Phone

The phone number to display in the footer.

Email

The email address to display in the footer.

Working Hours

Your opening hours, for example "Mon to Fri, 8am to 5pm".

Replace the logo

Online Store›Themes›Customize

  1. Open the editor. Follow the steps in Opening the Customize editor above.
  2. Go to the footer. Scroll the preview to the bottom of the page and click the footer, or find the Footer in the list on the left.
  3. Open the Logo info block. Click the Logo info block within the footer.
  4. Change the image. Under Logo image, remove the current image and upload your new logo file.
  5. Adjust the size. If needed, drag Custom logo width until the logo looks right.
  6. Save. Click Save in the top right.

Update your footer contact details

  1. Open the editor and go to the footer. As in the steps above.
  2. Open the Contacts block. Click the Contacts block within the footer.
  3. Edit the fields. Update the Address, Phone, Email and Working Hours as needed.
  4. Save. Click Save.
Do

When you want to change the links inside a footer column (not the heading or which menu it uses), edit them under Content › Menus. The footer columns are menu driven, exactly like the header and side menu. See Pages and navigation.

Take care

These areas appear on every page of your store, so a change here shows up everywhere at once. Preview carefully before saving, and after saving open your live site to confirm the footer, logo and menus look right on both desktop and mobile.
