Editing each page

The homepage

Your homepage is built from a stack of sections you can edit, reorder, and hide in the theme editor. This page walks through every section on it, top to bottom, and every setting inside each one.

Think of your homepage as a pile of building blocks laid one on top of the other. Each block is a section, and each section does one job: a rotating banner at the top, a row of picture tiles, a shelf of products, and so on. You do not need to touch any code. Everything here is edited by pointing and clicking in the theme editor.

How the homepage is built

Every section on the homepage is stacked in a single column, top to bottom, and you edit them all in the same place: the theme editor.

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize. Your homepage appears in a live preview on the right, with a list of its sections on the left.
  2. Pick a section to edit. Click any section name in the left-hand list, or click the matching area in the preview. Its settings open on the left.
  3. Reorder a section. In the left-hand list, drag a section up or down by the handle to change where it sits on the page.
  4. Hide a section. Click the eye icon next to a section name to hide it from customers without deleting it. Click the eye again to bring it back.
  5. Save to publish. Click Save at the top right. Nothing you change is visible to customers until you press Save.
Good to know

Most sections have a group of Section margin or Section spacing settings at the bottom. These control the empty space above and below the section, with separate values for desktop and mobile. If two sections feel too crowded or too far apart, this is where you adjust it.

Slideshow

The slideshow is the large rotating banner at the very top of the homepage. Each slide is a full-width image (or video) with optional heading, text, and a button, and it is your best spot to feature a promotion or a key product range.

Slideshow settings

These settings apply to the whole slideshow, not to one slide.

SettingWhat it does
Header overlayLets the top navigation bar sit on top of the first slide instead of pushing the slideshow down. Turn on for a fuller edge-to-edge look.
Header transparentMakes the header background see-through over the slideshow, so the image shows behind your menu.
Header transparent color schemeChooses the set of colors (text and icons) used by the header while it is transparent, so it stays readable against your image.
Use Logo image 2 for Header transparentSwaps in your alternate logo (a lighter version, for example) while the header is transparent over a dark image.
Section layoutChooses how wide the slideshow sits: full width or contained within the page margins.
Text positionSets where the heading and button appear on the slide (for example left, center, or right) on desktop.
Pagination positionSets where the little slide dots or navigation controls appear.
Aspect ratio (Image options)Sets the shape of the slide on desktop, such as tall, wide, or standard. This decides how much of each image is shown.
Aspect ratio (Mobile layout)The same shape control, but for phones, so slides can be taller on mobile than on desktop.
Text position (Mobile layout)Sets where the heading and button sit on a slide when viewed on a phone.
Enable autoplayMakes the slides advance on their own. Turn off if you want customers to click through manually.
Disable sliding on hoverPauses the automatic sliding while a visitor's mouse rests on the slideshow, so they can read it.
Speed slidingHow quickly one slide animates into the next.
Delay slidingHow long each slide stays on screen before the next one appears.
Sliding effectsThe style of transition between slides, such as a slide across or a fade.
Text animationAdds a gentle entrance animation to the heading and text as each slide appears.
Good to know

The four Section margin settings (Top, Bottom, Mobile top, Mobile bottom) at the bottom of the section control the gap above and below the slideshow. The same set appears on most sections, so you will recognize it everywhere.

Slide settings (per slide)

Each slide is a block inside the slideshow. Click a slide in the left-hand list to open its own settings.

Imageimage

The main picture shown on the slide for desktop screens.

Mobile imageimage

An optional separate picture used on phones. Useful when your desktop image is wide and would crop badly on a narrow screen.

Videovideo

An optional video to play in the slide instead of a still image.

Color schemecolor scheme

The set of colors used for this slide's text and button, so they stay readable against the image.

Subheadingtext

A short line of small text above the main heading, such as a category or a tagline.

Headingtext

The large headline on the slide. You can apply bold or a link to parts of it.

Descriptionrich text

A longer paragraph below the heading, with basic formatting.

Text widthselect

How wide the text block is allowed to spread across the slide.

Label (Button options)text

The words shown on the button, for example "Shop now". Leave blank to hide the button.

Link (Button options)link

Where the button takes the customer, such as a collection or a product page.

Show button dot (Button options)checkbox

Adds a small decorative dot to the button style.

Button style (Button options)select

Chooses the look of the button, such as solid or outlined.

Enable (Overlay options)checkbox

Turns on a dark shade over the image so white text stays readable.

Opacity (Overlay options)range

How dark that shade is on desktop. Higher means darker.

Opacity for mobile (Overlay options)range

The same darkness control, set separately for phones.

Change a slide image

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Open the slide. Click Slideshow in the left-hand list, then click the slide you want to change.
  3. Replace the image. Under Image, click Select image and choose a new picture, or upload one from your computer.
  4. Check the mobile view. If the image looks cropped on phones, add a separate Mobile image.
  5. Save. Click Save at the top right.

Banner tiles

Banner tiles are a row of up to four clickable picture cards, each pointing to a page or collection. They work well as quick shortcuts to your main product areas.

Section settings

This section has only the four Section margin settings: Top, Bottom, Mobile top, and Mobile bottom. They set the empty space above and below the row of tiles, with separate values for desktop and phones.

Tile settings (per tile)

Each tile is a block. You can have up to four. Click a tile in the left-hand list to edit it.

SettingWhat it does
Background colorThe fill color shown behind the tile, used when there is no image or around it.
SubheadingA short line of small text above the heading.
HeadingThe main title on the tile.
LabelThe words for the tile's link, such as "Shop range".
LinkWhere the tile takes the customer when clicked.
ImageThe picture shown on the tile.

Edit a tile and its link

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Open a tile. Click Banner tiles, then click the tile you want to change.
  3. Set the words and picture. Fill in the Heading, Label, and Image.
  4. Point it somewhere. In Link, choose the collection or page the tile should open.
  5. Save. Click Save.

Multicolumn

The multicolumn section is a tidy row of short columns, each with an icon, a heading, a little text, and an optional button. It is ideal for a "Why buy from us" strip or a set of service highlights.

Section settings

SettingWhat it does
Heading (Section heading options)The main title shown above the columns.
Heading sizeHow large that title appears.
SubheadingA short line of text above or near the heading.
Show subheading dotAdds a small decorative dot next to the subheading.
Columns per row (Section Content)How many columns sit side by side on desktop.
Content alignmentWhether the text in each column is left, center, or right aligned.
Enable swipe (Mobile layout)On phones, lets customers swipe the columns sideways instead of stacking them all vertically.
Card heading size (Heading options)The text size of each column's own heading.
Card layoutThe visual style of each column card.

The usual four Section margin settings appear at the bottom.

Column settings (per column)

Each column is a block. You can have up to twelve. Click a column in the left-hand list to edit it.

Iconselect

Chooses a small icon shown at the top of the column from a built-in set.

Headingtext

The column's title.

Descriptionrich text

A short paragraph of text under the heading.

Label (Button options)text

The button text. Leave blank to hide the button.

Link (Button options)link

Where the button leads.

Show button dot (Button options)checkbox

Adds a small decorative dot to the button.

Button style (Button options)select

The look of the button, such as solid or outlined.

Overlay linkcheckbox

Makes the whole column clickable, not just the button, so customers can tap anywhere on it.

Edit a column's text

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Open a column. Click Multicolumn, then click the column you want.
  3. Update it. Change the Icon, Heading, and Description.
  4. Save. Click Save.

Category grid

The category grid is a neat grid of picture cards, each with a title and a link, meant to guide customers into your main product categories.

Section settings

SettingWhat it does
Color schemeThe set of colors used for the section's background and text.
HeadingThe title shown above the grid.
Columns (desktop)How many category cards sit side by side on desktop.

The usual four Section margin settings appear at the bottom.

Category settings (per card)

Each card is a block. You can have up to thirty. Click a category in the left-hand list to edit it.

SettingWhat it does
ImageThe picture shown on the card.
TitleThe category name shown on the card.
LinkThe collection or page the card opens.

Add a category card

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Open the section. Click Category grid in the left-hand list.
  3. Add a block. Click Add category at the bottom of its block list.
  4. Fill it in. Set the Image, Title, and Link.
  5. Save. Click Save.

Product sliders

A product slider is a horizontal shelf of product cards that customers can scroll through, great for featuring a collection or a hand-picked set of products. The homepage has two of these, so you can showcase two different groups (for example "Best sellers" and "New arrivals"). Both sliders share the same settings, listed below.

Section settings

SettingWhat it does
Columns per rowHow many product cards are visible at once on desktop.
Heading (Section heading options)The title above the slider, such as "Best sellers".
Heading sizeHow large that title appears.
SubheadingA short line of text near the heading.
Show subheading dotAdds a small decorative dot next to the subheading.
Show borderAdds a dividing line for the heading area.
CollectionPick a collection and the slider fills itself with those products automatically.
ProductsInstead of a whole collection, hand-pick a specific list of products to show.
Enable autoplay (Slider options)Makes the shelf scroll on its own.
Disable sliding on hoverPauses the automatic scroll while a visitor's mouse is over it.
Show arrowsShows left and right arrows so customers can scroll manually.
Speed slidingHow quickly the shelf moves between positions.
Delay slidingHow long it waits before scrolling on its own again.
Button styleThe look of any button in the section.
Image ratio (Product card)The shape of each product image, such as square or tall.
Image fitWhether the product image fills the card or fits fully inside it.
Show collection nameShows the collection each product belongs to on its card.
Show secondary imageShows the product's second photo when a customer hovers over the card.
Show SKUShows the product's item code on the card.
Show vendorShows the brand or manufacturer on the card.
Show descriptionShows a short product description on the card.

The usual four Section margin settings appear at the bottom.

Good to know

You can fill a slider in one of two ways: choose a Collection to show everything in it automatically, or use Products to hand-pick an exact list. If you set both, the hand-picked list takes priority.

Edit a product slider's heading and products

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Pick the right slider. There are two Product slider sections in the left-hand list. Click the one you want to change.
  3. Set the heading. Type a new Heading, such as "New arrivals".
  4. Choose what shows. Pick a Collection, or use Products to hand-pick items.
  5. Save. Click Save.

Trust badges

The trust badges section is a row of small logos or badges (for example accreditations or payment marks) that reassure customers you are a trusted supplier.

Section settings

SettingWhat it does
SubheadingA short line of text above the heading.
HeadingThe title above the badges, such as "Trusted by".
Heading sizeHow large that title appears.

This section uses Section spacing rather than margin: Top spacing, Bottom spacing, Mobile top spacing, and Mobile bottom spacing. They do the same job, setting the empty space above and below the section for desktop and phones.

Badge settings (per badge)

Each badge is a block. You can have up to six. Click a badge in the left-hand list to edit it.

SettingWhat it does
Badge imageThe logo or badge picture to show.
Alt textA short description of the badge for screen readers and for when the image cannot load. Describe what the badge is, for example "ISO certified".

Swap a badge image

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Open a badge. Click Trust badges, then click the badge you want.
  3. Replace it. Under Badge image, select or upload a new picture, and update the Alt text to match.
  4. Save. Click Save.

Trustpilot

The Trustpilot section shows your live customer reviews from Trustpilot on the homepage. Most of its settings come from your Trustpilot account and are set once during setup, so you rarely need to change them.

Section settings

SettingWhat it does
HeadingThe title shown above the reviews, such as "What our customers say".
Business Unit IDThe code that connects this section to your Trustpilot account. It comes from Trustpilot and is set during setup.
Widget template IDTells Trustpilot which review layout to show. Also provided by Trustpilot.
Trustpilot review domainYour website address as registered with Trustpilot.
LocaleThe language and region for the reviews display, for example en-US.
Widget themeChooses a light or dark look for the review box.
Widget heightHow tall the review box is on the page.
Color schemeThe set of colors used for the section's background and text.
Take care

The Business Unit ID, Widget template ID, and Trustpilot review domain come straight from your Trustpilot account. Changing them can stop reviews from showing. It is safe to edit the Heading, Widget theme, and Widget height freely.

Change the reviews heading

  1. Open the editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Open the section. Click Trustpilot in the left-hand list.
  3. Edit the words. Type a new Heading. Leave the ID fields alone.
  4. Save. Click Save.
