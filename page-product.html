Editing each page

The product page

Every product page is built from the same set of blocks: the title, price, variant picker, buy buttons, documents, and more. This page explains each block and its settings, and how the page differs for build-your-own configurator products.

There are two different things people mean when they say "edit the product page", and it helps to keep them apart.

The layout is the arrangement of blocks on the page: which pieces appear, in what order, and how each one is set up. You edit the layout once, in the theme editor, and your choice applies to every product at the same time. That is what this page covers.

The content is the words and pictures for one particular item: its title, price, photos, description, and stock. You edit that on the product itself, under the Products area of your admin. For anything to do with a single product's own details, please head to the Products page.

Good to know

A quick way to remember it: if a change should affect all products (for example, hiding the SKU everywhere), you make it in the theme editor described here. If a change affects one product (for example, a new price for a single hose), you make it on that product under Products. See the Products page for that.

Opening the product page in the theme editor

All the layout settings on this page live in the theme editor. Because every product shares one layout, you open any single product to edit the template, and your changes apply across the whole store.

  1. Open the theme editor. Go to Online Store›Themes›Customize.
  2. Switch to a product page. At the very top of the editor there is a page dropdown that usually starts on your homepage. Open it, choose Products, then pick Default product. The editor now shows a real product so you can see your changes in a true preview.
  3. Find the Product information section. In the left-hand list of sections, click Product information. Its blocks open up beneath it. Each block is one part of the page.
  4. Click a block to edit it. Selecting a block shows its settings on the left. Change what you need.
  5. Save. Press Save at the top right. Nothing goes live until you save.
Take care

Layout changes here apply to all products at once. Preview the page before you save, and remember that removing a block (say, the price) removes it from every product page in the store.

The blocks on a product page

The Product information section is made up of blocks. Each block below is a part of the page you can turn on, turn off, reorder, and set up. Here is every block and exactly what its settings do.

Text

A small line of free text you can place anywhere among the other blocks, for a short note or label.

Texttext

The words shown. Type whatever short line you want to display.

Heading

The product title, taken automatically from the product's name. You do not type the title here; you only choose how large it looks.

Heading sizechoice

How large the product title appears on the page. Pick from the size options offered.

Good to know

To change the actual title wording, edit the product under Products. See the Products page.

Description

Shows the product's description text on the page. The words come from each product's own description field.

Descriptiontext

Controls how the description is shown for the block. The description wording itself is set per product under Products.

When on, a small "Tax included" note appears under the price. Turn it on only if your prices already include tax.

When on, a note tells customers that shipping is worked out at checkout.

Displays the product's SKU (its stock code) on the page. This block has no settings of its own. Turn it on to show the SKU, off to hide it. The SKU value itself is set per product.

Shows the stock status, such as "In stock" or "Sold out".

The chooser customers use to select an option, such as a size or a colour, on products that offer choices.

Reordering, adding, and hiding blocks

You arrange blocks in the same left-hand list where you find them. Here are the everyday edits.

Reorder the blocks on the product page

  1. Open the theme editor at Online Store›Themes›Customize and switch to a product page.
  2. Open Product information in the left-hand list so its blocks show.
  3. Drag a block up or down. Grab the block by the handle beside its name and drag it to its new spot. The preview updates as you move it.
  4. Save.

Turn a block on or off

  1. Find the block in the Product information list.
  2. Use the eye icon. Click the small eye beside the block name to hide it, or click again to show it. Hidden blocks stay in the list so you can bring them back later.
  3. Save.

Add a new block

  1. Open Product information in the left-hand list.
  2. Click "Add block" at the bottom of its block list.
  3. Pick the block you want from the menu (for example, Certification Images).
  4. Set it up and place it. Fill in its settings, then drag it to where you want it.
  5. Save.
Good to know

Hiding a block with the eye icon is safer than removing it. A hidden block keeps all its settings, so you can switch it back on any time without setting it up again.

Whole-page settings

Besides the blocks, the Product information section itself has settings that affect the whole page. Click the section name (not a block) to reach them. Most are set once at launch and rarely need changing, but here is what the main groups do.

Configurator ConfiguratorThe heading and description shown above the option builder on build-your-own products, and which collection supplies the gas type choices. See the Configurator products page.
Part Number LookupConnection details for the part-number lookup feature. Set up by your Makro contact; leave as is.
Request a Quote APIConnection details for the quote feature. Set up by your Makro contact; leave as is.
Color scheme, Header overlay, Floating bottom barThe colour scheme for the page, whether the header sits over the top of the page, and whether a floating bar shows at the bottom on mobile.
MediaHow the product photo gallery is laid out: its position on desktop and mobile, the gallery style, and how images fit their frames.

Please leave the Part Number Lookup and Request a Quote API details alone. They contain connection settings that link your store to background services. Changing them can stop those features working.

Recommended products

Further down is a Recommended products section. It works just like Complementary products and has the very same settings (layout and slider, section heading, product card, and section margin). The difference is where the items come from: Shopify picks recommendations automatically, based on what customers tend to buy together, rather than you choosing them by hand. Adjust its settings the same way, by clicking the section in the theme editor.

Specifications

Many products also show a Specifications section, listing technical details in a tidy layout. Its settings are lighter than the others.

It also has the standard Section margin settings for spacing on desktop and mobile.

Good to know

The specification content itself (the actual list of details) is set per product under Products. See the Products page for how to edit it.

Configurator products are different

Most products use the plain Variant picker block described above: the customer picks from a short menu of ready-made choices. Build-your-own Configurator products behave differently. Instead of a plain picker, they show an option builder: several menus the customer combines step by step to create exactly the item they need, such as a hose built from a gas type, length, and end fittings.

This is why the Product information section has a Configurator group of settings (its heading, description, and gas type collection). Those settings only affect configurator products, and the option builder replaces the ordinary variant picker on those pages.

Do

Configurator products have their own rules for how the options, prices, and part numbers are built, and they are not edited like ordinary products. For everything about them, please read the dedicated Configurator products page.
