Editing your store

Pages and navigation

How to edit your content pages, the menus that guide shoppers around the site, and the product categories (collections).

Jump straight to this in your store
Edit the mega menu All menus Pages Collections
These open your Shopify admin in a new browser tab. You may be asked to log in. For the full list, see Store quick-links.

This part of your store is all about the words and links that surround your products. Your pages hold standalone content like your company story and contact details. Your menus are the clickable links that help shoppers find their way around. Your collections are the category pages that group related products together. None of this touches the products themselves, so it is a friendly place to start making the site your own.

Everything below is done from your Shopify admin. Each task lists exactly where to click, and you can always undo a change by editing again and saving. Take your time, and remember that saving is what makes a change go live.

Your content pages

A page is a piece of standalone content that is not a product. Think of the parts of a website that describe your business rather than sell an item: your story, how to reach you, your terms. Each page has its own web address and can be linked to from a menu so shoppers can find it.

Here are the content pages your store already has:

PageWhat it is
About USThe company story.
ContactContact details and a form shoppers can use to reach you.
FaqFrequently asked questions and their answers.
Terms & ConditionsYour terms.
Become a WT Farley DealerInformation for people who want to sign up as a dealer.
Your Privacy ChoicesThe privacy opt-out page.

How to edit a page

Content›Pages

  1. Open your pages. In the left menu of your admin, click Content, then click Pages. You will see a list of all your pages.
  2. Choose a page. Click the name of the page you want to change. It opens in the editor.
  3. Edit the title. The Title field at the top is the page's headline. Change the wording here if you want a different name shown to shoppers.
  4. Edit the content. The large Content box below is a rich text editor. Type directly into it, and use the toolbar to make text bold, add headings, create bullet lists, or add links, much like a word processor.
  5. Check the search listing (optional). Lower down you may see a Search engine listing section. This controls the short title and description that appears in search results. You can edit it, but leaving it as is works well too.
  6. Confirm it is visible. Make sure Visibility is set to Visible so shoppers can see the page.
  7. Save. Click Save in the top right. Your changes are now live.
Take care

In your list of pages there is also one called "page template". This is not real content, it is a layout the theme uses behind the scenes. Please do not edit or delete it, or parts of the site may stop displaying correctly.

Store policies

Your store policies are separate from the content pages above. These are the standard legal statements shoppers expect to find: your Refund policy, Shipping policy, Privacy policy, and Terms of service. Shopify keeps them in their own place, and they appear at web addresses that begin with /policies/. Links to them usually sit in the footer of your site.

How to edit a policy

Settings›Policies

  1. Open your settings. Click Settings at the very bottom of the left menu in your admin.
  2. Go to policies. Click Policies. You will see a box for each policy.
  3. Edit the text. Click into the policy you want to change and type or paste your wording. This is the same kind of rich text editor as your pages.
  4. Save. Click Save for that policy. It is now published at its /policies/ address.
Good to know

If a policy is empty and you are not sure where to begin, Shopify can create a starting template for you. Look for the option to insert or create a template inside each policy box, then adjust the wording to match your business. Always read a generated template carefully before saving, since it is only a starting point.

Menus and navigation

Menus are the sets of clickable links that guide shoppers around your site: the links across the top, the categories in the big product menu, and the columns of links in the footer. You edit them all in one place.

Good to know

When you open a menu, each link is shown as a row with a small handle on the left (a set of dots or lines you can grab). Items that sit slightly indented under another item are its sub-items, which is how drop-down and nested navigation is built.

How to add a menu item

  1. Open the menu. Go to Content › Menus and click the name of the menu you want to change.
  2. Add the item. Click Add menu item.
  3. Name it. Type the text you want shoppers to see, for example "Regulators".
  4. Link it. In the link field, choose what the item should point to: a collection (a product category), a page, or a web address you type in. A list of suggestions appears as you click, so you can pick from your existing collections and pages.
  5. Save. Click Save menu.

How to rename or reorder items

  1. Rename. Click an item to open it, change its name or link, then save the menu.
  2. Reorder. Grab the handle on the left of an item and drag it up or down into the position you want.
  3. Save. Click Save menu when the order looks right.

How to create a sub-item

  1. Drag it right. Grab an item by its handle and drag it slightly to the right, so it tucks under the item directly above it. It becomes a sub-item of that item.
  2. Save. Click Save menu. The item now appears nested underneath its parent.
Take care

The sidebar Menu is your main product navigation, so shoppers rely on it every day. After you edit it, open your live site and click the links you changed to confirm each one lands on the right page.

Product categories (collections)

A collection is a category page that groups related products together, for example all your oxygen regulators on one page. Your store has about 91 collections, which is what makes it easy for shoppers to browse by type of equipment.

Here is the most important idea to understand: most of your collections fill themselves automatically, based on the Tags on your products. A tag is a short label you add to a product. For example, the "Regulators - Oxygen" collection automatically gathers every product that carries the tag "Regulators - Oxygen". You do not add products to it by hand, you simply tag the product and it appears.

How to view your collections

Products›Collections

  1. Open collections. Click Products in the left menu, then click Collections. You will see the full list.

How to edit a collection

  1. Open it. Click the collection's name in the list.
  2. Edit the details. Change the Title, write or update the Description shown at the top of the category page, and set the Image used to represent the collection.
  3. Choose the sort order. Pick how products are ordered on the page, for example by best selling, by price, or alphabetically.
  4. Save. Click Save.
Good to know

There are two kinds of collection. An automated collection (sometimes called a smart collection) fills itself using conditions on tags, so products flow in and out on their own as you tag them. A manual collection is one you fill by adding products to it by hand. Most of your collections are automated.

Do

To put a product into a category, add the matching Tag to that product. This is the everyday way to organise your catalogue, and it keeps your automated collections accurate.

How to add a product to a category

  1. Open the product. Go to Products, then click the product you want to categorise. (See the Products page of this guide for more on editing products.)
  2. Add the tag. In the Tags box, type the exact tag that matches the collection, for example "Regulators - Oxygen", and add it.
  3. Save. Click Save. The product now appears in the matching collection automatically.
Take care

When editing a collection you may see a Conditions area, which sets the tag rules that decide what belongs. Changing these rules can accidentally empty a collection or pull in the wrong products. Unless you are confident, leave the conditions alone and organise products by changing their tags instead.

Homepage and store design

Your homepage and the overall look of the store are edited with the theme customizer. This is a visual, drag-and-drop editor with a live preview, so you can see your changes as you make them before anything goes public.

Online Store›Themes›Customize

  1. Open the customizer. Click Online Store, then Themes, then the Customize button.
  2. Find a section. The page is built from named sections, for example a banner or a featured products row. Click a section in the left panel, or click it directly in the preview, to open its settings.
  3. Edit safely. You can change text, swap images, and update banners within a section. Your edits appear instantly in the preview.
  4. Save. Click Save in the top right when you are happy. Your changes go live on the site.
Good to know

The customizer shows your real homepage on the right and a list of its sections on the left. Because it is a preview, nothing you try is public until you press Save, so it is a safe place to experiment.

Do not

Near the Customize button you may notice an Edit code option. That opens the store's underlying programming, which is best left to your developer. Editing code by mistake can break how the site looks or works, so please stay in the Customize editor.
