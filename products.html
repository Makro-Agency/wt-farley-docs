Editing your store

Products

How to edit your everyday products: prices, photos, stock, descriptions, categories, and whether a product is visible. Build-your-own configurator products have their own section.

Your products are the heart of your store. This page walks you through everything you will do day to day: changing a price, swapping a photo, updating a description, marking something out of stock, and adding brand new items. You do not need any technical knowledge. Take your time, follow the numbered steps, and remember that nothing goes live to customers until you press Save.

Every product lives in the same place in your admin. To get there, look at the left-hand menu and follow the path shown throughout this guide.

The three kinds of product

Before you edit anything, it helps to know that your store has three kinds of product. They all live in the same list, but they behave a little differently. Knowing which kind you are looking at tells you what you can safely change.

TypeWhat it isExample
Simple product One item, one price, one SKU. The most common kind, and the easiest to edit. Hose Splicer (SKU MF-7041, $12.00)
Variant product One product with a few choices, where each choice has its own price and SKU. Core SP Cart with White Drawers, with the option "Add Drawer Organizers to your Cart"
Configurator product Configurator A build-your-own product with many option menus that customers combine to create exactly what they need. Oxygen Hose
Good to know

Configurator products work very differently from the other two, and they have their own set of rules. This page covers simple and variant products. For anything marked with the Configurator pill, please head to the Configurator products page instead.

Finding a product

Whenever you want to edit something, the first step is always the same: find the product and open it.

  1. Open your product list. In the left-hand menu, go to Products›All products. You will see every product in your store, listed row by row.
  2. Search by name or SKU. Use the search box at the top of the list. Type part of the product name (for example "hose") or the SKU, and the list narrows down as you type.
  3. Filter to narrow the list. You can filter the list by status (for example, show only Active or only Draft products) or by product type. This is handy when you have a lot of products.
  4. Open the product. Click the product name to open its editor. This is where all the changes happen.
Good to know

The product list shows a few columns at a glance: a small thumbnail photo, the product name, its status (Active or Draft), the inventory count, the product type, and the vendor. If a column looks blank, it simply means that detail has not been filled in for that product yet.

Editing a simple product

This is the part you will use most often. When you open a simple product, the editor is divided into clearly labelled sections. You do not have to touch every section every time. Change only what you need, then Save. Here is what each section does.

Title and description

The Title is the product name customers see. The Description is the main text on the product page. The description uses a rich text editor, which works like a simple word processor: you can make text bold, add links, and create bulleted lists using the little toolbar above the box. Write in plain, friendly language that helps a customer understand the product.

Media (photos)

The Media section holds your product photos. To add a photo, click the upload area and choose an image from your computer, or drag the file straight onto it. To change the order, drag a photo into a new position. To remove one, select it and delete it. The first image is the main photo that shows on listings and at the top of the product page, so make sure your best image sits first.

Pricing

The Pricing section has three fields worth knowing:

  • Price: the amount the customer pays.
  • Compare-at price: an optional original price. When you set it higher than the price, the store shows the original with a line through it, so customers can see the saving.
  • Cost per item: what the product costs you. This is private, is never shown to customers, and simply helps you track profit.

Inventory

The Inventory section is where you record stock:

  • SKU: your internal code for the product, for example MF-7041.
  • Track quantity: a switch that tells Shopify to count stock for you.
  • Quantity on hand: how many you have.
Take care

When "Track quantity" is switched on and the quantity reaches 0, the product can show as sold out and customers may not be able to buy it. If a product should always be available, either keep the quantity topped up or leave tracking off for that item.

Status

A product's status decides whether customers can see it. Active means it is live and visible in your store. Draft means it is hidden while you work on it. Set a product to Draft while you prepare it, then switch it to Active when it is ready to sell.

Product organization

The Product organization section is more important than it looks, because it controls where your product appears on the site.

  • Product type: a simple category label, such as "Hose".
  • Vendor: the brand. For your store this is usually WT Farley.
  • Tags: short keywords attached to the product.
  • Collections: groups the product belongs to.
Do

Tags are what place a product into your category pages automatically. When you add the right tag, the product shows up on the matching category page on its own. So whenever you add or edit a product, make sure it has the correct tags. To see how tags map to category pages, read the Pages & navigation page.

Search engine listing (SEO)

Near the bottom of the editor is a section called Search engine listing. This controls the title and short description that appear when your product shows up in a Google search. You can leave it as is, and Shopify will use your product title and description automatically. If you want to fine-tune how a product reads in search results, edit the page title and description here.

And always: Save

No change takes effect until you press Save. Get into the habit of saving before you leave a page. If you navigate away without saving, Shopify will usually warn you.

Worked example: change a price

  1. Open the product. Go to Products›All products, search for the product, and click its name.
  2. Find the Pricing section. Scroll to Pricing.
  3. Type the new price. Click the Price field, clear the old amount, and enter the new one.
  4. Save. Press Save. The new price is live straight away.

Worked example: swap the main photo

  1. Open the product from Products›All products.
  2. Go to the Media section.
  3. Add the new photo. Upload it or drag it into the Media area.
  4. Make it first. Drag the new photo to the front of the row so it becomes the main image.
  5. Tidy up. If you no longer need the old photo, select it and delete it.
  6. Save. Press Save.

The extra fields (metafields)

Below the standard fields, each product has some extra custom fields that were set up specially for your store. They are sometimes called metafields. They power things like the wholesale price, the Specifications area, and downloadable spec sheets. The ones below are safe for you to edit.

custom.wtfarley_b2b_pricemoney

The wholesale price shown to approved B2B (business) customers. Safe to edit. Enter the amount just like a normal price.

custom.specification_contentrich text

The Specifications shown on the product page. Safe to edit. Use the rich text tools to add headings, lists, and bold text.

custom.documentsfiles

Spec sheets and manuals offered for download on the product page. Safe to edit. Upload or remove files here.

custom.card_descriptiontext

The short line shown on the product card in listings. Safe to edit. Keep it brief and clear.

custom.mri_conditionaltrue/false

Marks a product as MRI safe. Safe to edit. Set it to true when the product is MRI safe.

custom.gas_typetext

The gas the product is for, such as Oxygen or Air. Safe to edit.

custom.max_order_qtynumber

An optional cap on how many of this item a customer can order at once. Safe to edit. Leave it blank for no cap.

Do not

On normal products, please leave these fields alone. They belong to configurator products or are reference values from your old website, and changing them can break how a product behaves: custom.is_configurable, custom.sku_prefix, custom.option_groups, custom.excluded_choices, anything starting with custom.cfg_, and custom.s4s_catalog_id and custom.s4s_created_at.

Variant products

A variant product is one product that offers a few choices, where each choice is its own sellable item with its own price and SKU. A good example is the Core SP Cart with White Drawers. It has the option "Add Drawer Organizers to your Cart", with choices such as Cart Only at $1,286 and four organizer add-ons at $1,333 each. When you open the product, you will see each of these listed as a variant.

To edit the price or SKU of one variant:

  1. Open the product from Products›All products.
  2. Find the Variants section and click the variant you want to change.
  3. Update the price or SKU in the fields shown for that variant.
  4. Save.

To add a new choice (option value):

  1. Open the product and go to the Variants (options) section.
  2. Add a new value to the existing option, for example a new organizer choice.
  3. Set its price and SKU so the new choice is ready to sell.
  4. Save.

To assign a specific photo to a variant:

  1. Add the photo to the product's Media section first.
  2. Open the variant and choose the image you want linked to it.
  3. Save. That photo now shows when a customer selects that choice.
Take care

Each variant is a real item a customer can buy, so keep every variant's price and SKU correct. A blank or wrong price on one variant means customers may pay the wrong amount for that choice.

Products with colour or style swatches

Some products show clickable swatches for their choices instead of a plain drop-down menu. These little colour or style buttons are powered by a "Color" setting on the product's options. To change which choices appear, edit the product's options and variants exactly as described above. The look of the swatches themselves is handled by the theme, so you do not need to adjust any design settings. Add or remove the choice, and the swatch follows.

Adding a new product

When you have a brand new item to sell, you build it from scratch. Here is the full process.

  1. Start a new product. Go to Products›Add product.
  2. Add the title and description. Give it a clear name and write the product description.
  3. Add media. Upload your photos, and make sure the best one sits first.
  4. Set the price. Enter the Price, and a Compare-at price if you want to show a saving.
  5. Set up inventory. Add the SKU, and switch on Track quantity with a starting quantity if you want Shopify to count stock.
  6. Fill in organization and tags. Set the product type and vendor, and add the correct tags so the product lands on the right category pages.
  7. Choose the status. Set it to Draft while you finish, or Active to make it live.
  8. Save.
Good to know

To match an existing product's setup, it is often quicker to use Duplicate than to start from a blank product. You get all the same fields and tags, ready to adjust. See the next section for how.

Duplicating and deleting

Duplicating creates a copy you can tweak, which saves a lot of setup time. Deleting removes a product for good, so use it carefully.

To duplicate a product:

  1. Open the product you want to copy from Products›All products.
  2. Choose Duplicate. Use the Duplicate option at the top right of the product page.
  3. Rename and adjust. Give the copy its own title, then update the price, photos, SKU, and tags.
  4. Set the status and Save. Set it to Active when ready.

To remove a product:

  1. Open the product.
  2. Set it to Draft to hide it, or use the Archive option to tuck it away while keeping its history.
  3. Delete only if you are sure. The Delete option removes the product permanently.
Take care

Prefer Draft or Archive over Delete. Draft and Archive hide the product but keep its details, so you can bring it back. Deleting is permanent and cannot be undone.
