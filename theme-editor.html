Editing each page

The theme editor

The theme editor is the visual, drag-and-drop tool where you build and adjust every page of your store. Learn it once here, and the page-by-page guides that follow will make sense straight away.

Nearly everything visual on your storefront is edited in one place: the theme editor, also called the customizer. It shows a live preview of your site and lets you change text, images, layout, colours, and fonts without touching any code.

Opening the theme editor

  1. Go to your themes. In the admin, open Online Store›Themes
  2. Find your live theme. The theme currently powering wtfarley.com is marked as your live theme at the top.
  3. Click Customize. Press the Customize button next to it. The theme editor opens with a live preview of your store.
Good to know

You can jump straight in from the customer side too: while viewing any page in the theme editor, it shows exactly what shoppers see, and updates instantly as you change settings.

The parts of the editor

The theme editor has the same layout wherever you are:

  • The page selector runs across the top. Use it to switch which page you are editing, for example the homepage, a product page, or the cart. This is important: settings you change apply to the page type shown here.
  • The sections list sits down one side. It lists every section stacked on the current page, in top-to-bottom order.
  • The live preview fills the middle. It is your real store, and it updates as you edit.
  • The settings panel appears when you click a section or block. This is where you change text, images, and options.
  • The top bar holds the device preview (desktop or mobile), undo and redo, and the Save button.
Good to know

There is a gear or settings icon (often at the bottom of the sections list) for Theme settings. Those control the whole store at once, such as colours and fonts, and are covered at the end of this page.

Sections, blocks, and settings

Three simple words unlock the whole editor.

Sectiona page band

A horizontal band of a page, for example a banner, a product row, or the footer. Pages are built by stacking sections. You can add, reorder, hide, and remove them.

Blockan item inside a section

A smaller item within a section, for example one slide in a slideshow or one column in a row. Blocks can also be added, reordered, and removed inside their section.

Settinga single option

One adjustable option on a section or block, for example its heading text, an image, a link, or an on/off switch. The page-by-page guides list every setting for you.

Good to know

Your header, footer, and side menu are shown on every page. They live in special section groups, so editing them once updates them everywhere. They have their own guide: see Header, menu and footer.

The everyday actions

Edit a section or block

  1. Click it. Click the section in the list, or click it directly in the preview. Its settings open.
  2. Change the settings. Update the text, image, link, or options.
  3. Watch the preview update as you go, then Save.

Add a section

  1. Choose the page from the page selector at the top.
  2. Click Add section in the sections list.
  3. Pick the section you want from the menu. It is added to the page.
  4. Fill in its settings and Save. The full list of available sections is in the Section catalog.

Reorder, hide, or remove

  1. Reorder: grab a section by the handle (the dots on its left) and drag it up or down.
  2. Hide: click the eye icon on a section to hide it from shoppers without deleting it. Click again to show it.
  3. Remove: open the section and choose Remove to delete it from the page. You can add it back later.
  4. Save when the page looks right.
Good to know

Made a change you do not like? Use the undo arrow in the top bar, or simply leave the page without saving and nothing sticks.

Saving, previewing, and publishing

Because you are editing your live theme, pressing Save makes the change public straight away. There is no separate publish step for edits to the live theme.

Do

Use the device preview (desktop and mobile icons) before saving, so you can check a change looks good on phones as well as computers. Most of your shoppers may be on mobile.

Take care

Since edits to the live theme go public immediately, make big or experimental changes on a copy first (next section), then publish the copy when it is ready.

Working safely with theme copies

Your Themes page holds your live theme plus a library of saved copies. Making a copy is the safe way to try something large, like redesigning the homepage, without affecting the live site.

  1. Duplicate first. On Online Store›Themes, find your live theme, open its actions menu, and choose Duplicate. This makes an exact copy.
  2. Edit the copy. Click Customize on the duplicate and make your changes there. The live site is untouched.
  3. Publish when ready. When the copy is exactly right, open its actions menu and choose Publish. It becomes the live theme, and the old one stays in your library as a backup.
Do not

Do not delete themes in your library without checking with your developer. They are your backups and history. Also avoid the Edit code option, which opens the store's programming and is meant for your developer.

Theme settings (the whole store at once)

Theme settings control choices that apply everywhere, not just one page. You reach them from the gear or Theme settings icon inside the editor. Here is what each group of settings does. You can change any of these and Save, and the change applies across the whole store.

Settings groupWhat it controls
Color schemeThe overall colour scheme of the site, and an optional dark or light mode toggle.
ColorsThe exact colours used across the store (backgrounds, text, buttons, accents).
TypographyThe fonts and text sizes for headings, body text, buttons, menus, and product cards.
Border radiusHow rounded the corners are on buttons, cards, forms, and badges.
CardProduct card behaviour, such as a hover effect and whether the brand name shows.
Color swatchHow colour options appear on products (as swatches), including custom colours.
Social mediaLinks to your social accounts (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, and others) used in the footer and elsewhere.
FaviconThe tiny logo shown on the browser tab.
Quick viewThe quick view and quick buy buttons on product cards, including on mobile.
SearchSearch behaviour, including predictive search and the product-card details shown in results. This group also holds the settings for your part-number Lookup feature. See Custom features.
Fullscreen menuAn optional fullscreen navigation menu with its own promotional blocks.
PaginationHow long product lists are split across pages, and the load-more button style.
ReviewsStyling for the Judge.me reviews app, if reviews are used.
CartThe cart type (drawer or page), the cart drawer wording, and an optional free-shipping progress message. See Cart and search.
Currency formatWhether currency codes are shown next to prices.
AdvancedExtras such as a back-to-top button and breadcrumb links.
Take care

Theme settings change the look of the entire store, so a small change here is felt everywhere. Change one thing at a time, check the preview on desktop and mobile, and if you are unsure about colours or fonts, try it on a duplicate theme first.
